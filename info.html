How to Use the Visualization Tool

This web-based tool uses analytical solutions to the Advection Dispersion (AD) equation for different scenarios and to graphically represent the various transport mechanisms. Certain values can be changed to study their effect on contaminant transport in groundwater including darcy velocity, diffusion coefficient, contaminant retardation and first-order decay parameter. Parameters

Effective porosity (ne): Porosity is the ratio of the volume of voids to the total volume of soil. Effective porosity is the porosity through which flow can occur (i.e. the pores have to be connected). Unconnected and dead-end pores are not included in the effective porosity [-].

Longitudinal dispersivity (αx): Using the assumption that mechanical dispersion is linearly related to average porewater velocity, longitudinal dispersity is defined as the ratio of longitudinal dispersion coefficient (dispersion in the direction of flow) to average porewater velocity. [L]. It represents the local variations in the groundwater velocity in the direction of flow.

Transverse dispersivity (αy): This is the ratio of transverse dispersion coefficient (dispersion normal to flow direction) to the average porewater velocity [L]

Concentration(c): The amount of solute (in moles or mass) per unit volume [M/L3]

Darcy velocity (q): The flow of fluid (here water) per unit cross-sectional area of soil [L/T].

Pore water (seepage) velocity(v): The flow of water across the unit cross-sectional area of pore space, [L/T] v = q/ne.

Effective diffusion coefficient (D*): Diffusion in porous media is slower than in water because the ions must take longer paths as they travel around mineral grains. In order to account for the impact of longer pathways, an effective diffusion coefficient is used, which is less than the diffusion coefficient in water [L2/T].

Time (t): Measure the time taken by a process [T].

Distance (x): The distance from coordinates in the direction of the x-axis [L].

Distance (y): The distance from coordinates in the direction of the y-axis [L].

Area (a): The cross-section through which groundwater is passing [L2]

Retardation factor (R): Some solutes will travel considerably more slowly through the aquifer than the groundwater that transports them due to sorption processes; this phenomenon is referred to as retardation. [-]

Half-life (λ): The time required for a substance to reduce to half its initial amount [T].
