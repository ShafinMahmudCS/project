How to Use the Visualizer

This website contains many visualizers to provide graphical representation of mechanisms. By the help of slider, values can be changed to study groundwater contamination under various subsurface conditions such as retardation and first-order decay.

Parameters

Effective porosity (ne): The porosity through which flow can occur. Noninterconnected and dead-end pores are not included in the effective porosity.

Longitudinal dispersivity (αx): The mixing that occurs along the direction of the flowpath because of invading solute-containing water which does not travel at the same velocity.

Transverse dispersivity (αy): The mixing that occurs normal to the direction of the flowpath because of invading solute-containing water that does not travel at the same velocity

Concentration(c): The amount of a substance present in a defined space.

Darcy velocity (q): The volume of flow through a unit of cross-sectional area

Pore water velocity(v): The rate at which the flux of water across the unit cross-sectional area of pore space occurs.

Effective diffusion coefficient (D*): Effective diffusion coefficient is based on the average cross-sectional area open to diffusion and the distance traveled by molecules in porous media.

Time (t): Measure the time taken by a process.

Distance (x): An amount of space between two things.

y: The y-axis

Area (a): The region bounded by the shape of an object

Retardation factor (R): Due to sorption processes, some solutes will move much more slowly through the aquifer than the ground water that is transporting them; this effect is called retardation

Half-life (λ): The time required for a quantity to reduce to half of its initial value.
